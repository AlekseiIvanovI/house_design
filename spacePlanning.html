Key aspects of space planning:

Functionality: The designer assesses the intended function of the space and determines how to best allocate and arrange different areas. This includes identifying the primary activities that will take place within the space and ensuring that the layout supports those activities effectively.

Traffic Flow: Space planning takes into account the movement patterns and circulation within the space. The designer considers factors such as entrances, exits, pathways, and access to key areas to ensure a smooth and logical flow of people throughout the space.

Furniture and Equipment Placement: The designer determines the optimal placement of furniture, fixtures, and equipment to maximize efficiency and convenience. This includes considering ergonomic principles, ensuring adequate clearances, and creating comfortable and functional arrangements.
